    Mount St. Piran

    This was my attempt to squeeze in one last hike before the hiking season “officially” ended. There had been a lot of snowfall in parts of Kananaskis but barely any in Lake Louise. Asking around resulted in various conflicting reports of avalanche and winter conditions. We decided to head out to the Lake Louise area and check the conditions out for ourselves. There was probably a foot of snow at higher elevations, but everything was otherwise very hikeable.

    Skyline Trail

    Skyline was probably the hike that first led me to backpacking. I discovered it two years ago in 2016⁄17 and since then, was obsessed with the thought of hiking it. Now that I’ve actually done it, it feels strange! The weather was good, the views were great, the company was awesome, I packed my gear really well (light), the only thing that wassn’t good were my knees. Date: 19-08-03 to 19-08-05
  • Jul 21
    Windtower

    Another “easy scramble” with high payoff. Nonstop views from the moment you get past treeline. Tried this hike last year and did not make it past West Wind Pass due to weather. Bit of easy scrambling/route finding after the pass. All trails eventually lead to the same place, and lots of people to follow if you get lost. Views of Spray Lake along the way are classic. All in all, my kind of hike!

  • Aug 19
    Ha Ling Peak and Miner’s Peak

    Short and super-popular. Steep going up with some scrambling at the top. Date: 2017-08-19 Location: Kananaskis Distance: 7 km Elevation gain: 740 m Time: 4 h, including some long breaks Friends: Mario, Chris No photos from the initial ascent, which was on a well-marked trail through forest. Shortly after clearing the treeline, it gets a lot rockier and steeper. Miner’s Peak is right beside Ha Ling Peak. All these people taking different routes to the summit.
