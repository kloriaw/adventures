    Nova Scotia

  • August 3
    Skyline Trail

    Skyline was probably the hike that first led me to backpacking. I discovered it two years ago in 2016⁄17 and since then, was obsessed with the thought of hiking it. Now that I’ve actually done it, it feels strange! The weather was good, the views were great, the company was awesome, I packed my gear really well (light), the only thing that wassn’t good were my knees. Date: 2019-08-03 to 2019-08-05
  • July 21
    Windtower

    Another “easy scramble” with high payoff. Nonstop views from the moment you get past treeline. Tried this hike last year and did not make it past West Wind Pass due to weather. Bit of easy scrambling/route finding after the pass. All trails eventually lead to the same place, and lots of people to follow if you get lost. Views of Spray Lake along the way are classic. All in all, my kind of hike!

  • September 22
    Healy Pass

    That day, a valiant attempt was made to backpack to Egypt Lake. It was also probably the most miserable, soggy, muddy, and nasty walk I’ve ever had. An entire day of trudging up small rivers in the chilly gloom. By the time we made it to the pass, the sunlight and gorgeous sights cheered us up, but we decided to head back since everyone’s shoes were wet at that point.
