Ha Ling Peak III

November 22, 2020 · 349 words · 2 minute read hiking canada

My first sunrise hike and it had to be a winter one. I was itching to get out after staying at home for too long. I decided that this would be a safe and easy objective and my hunch was correct. The third return to the well-trod Ha Ling. Getting up early helps avoid the crowds, although we still encountered a few groups at the summit.We started hiking in the dark at 6 am and made it to the summit just before sunrise. I overestimated my ability to bound up the last grind at the end and found myself huffing and puffing my way up there, as usual. I found it really easy to hike up in the dark with ultra-bright headlamps and microspikes - the trail was packed solid on the way up and mostly bare at the summit. My hands and feet froze for the half an hour we stayed at the summit…handwarmers were a lifesaver but my feet went painfully numb.

We were blessed with a beautiful sunrise, which made everything worth it. Not to say the hike was wasn’t fun on its own merit, although waking up at 4 am was definitely the most painful part of the experience. I was surprised by how much I enjoyed the brightening up of the skies prior to sunrise, just a tinge of lighter washed out blue on the horizon at first, then more and more snow-covered peaks appearing before me, somehow appearing both tack-sharp and watery. We broke through the treeline in time to be greeted by that mysterious presence known as blue hour. Being dwarfed/surrounded by giants while bathed in that pre-dawn half-light, was more humbling and magical than the actual sunrise that followed. It wasn’t the dazzling pastel hues exploding on the horizon that will stick with me (although that was quite a beautiful sight on its won), but the Brisk stillness, embodying the awakening of the whole world around you. The feeling of being on top of a mountain as the sun rises is indescribable.

Date: 2020-11-22

Location: Kananaskis

Friends: Chris, Jonathan
