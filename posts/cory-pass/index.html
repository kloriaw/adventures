Cory Pass

July 10, 2021 · 122 words · 1 minute read hiking Canada

The Cory Pass loop won first prize for the most mosquitoes I’ve ever encountered on day hike. (For some reason, backcountry hikes are in a whole other league when it comes to mosquito and horsefly numbers.) It was a beautiful day for us and for the mozzies!

Another great thing about this hike was the varied and cool terrain we encountered throughout. The area around Gargoyle Valley was the highlight of the hike.

Date: 2021-07-10

Location: Banff

Friends: Eric, Ivan, Kat

Aspen forests.

Pine forests.

Open meadows.

Light fun scrambling.

(Small) waterfalls.

Slippery slopes.

Mount Edith.

Gargoyles guarding the eponymous valley.

Breathtaking desolate landscapes.

Boulder fields.

Mount Louis.

A long gentle walk back in the company of mosquitoes. I loved this hike!
